ALLERGIC & INFLAMMATORY CONDITIONS

Pioneering new therapeutic frontiers for type 2 inflammation and related disorders

A balanced immune system is foundational to human well-being. However, complications arise when this system loses its equilibrium—either by launching an excessive, uncontrolled attack or by failing to mount an adequate defense. Our scientific investigations center on decoding the specific molecular and cellular triggers that cause immune dysregulation in allergic and inflammatory conditions. This dedication to immunological innovation has yielded a robust and expanding clinical pipeline, evaluating novel therapeutic avenues for allergies, autoimmune disorders, and even tumor immunology.

Dedicated to advancing the science of type 2 inflammation

We possess a deep-rooted legacy as pioneers in the study of type 2 inflammation. Extensive late-stage clinical trials have established that the IL-4 and IL-13 biological pathways are primary catalysts for this specific inflammatory response. Type 2 inflammation is a key underlying factor in a variety of interconnected, often overlapping conditions, such as severe asthma, atopic dermatitis, chronic obstructive pulmonary disease (COPD), chronic rhinosinusitis with nasal polyps, and prurigo nodularis.

To further revolutionize patient care, our ongoing research targets additional conditions driven by type 2 inflammation, including bullous pemphigoid, lichen simplex chronicus, and chronic pruritus of unknown origin.

Beyond type 2 pathways, we have extensively investigated the impact of the interleukin-6 (IL-6) pathway on persistent inflammatory diseases like polymyalgia rheumatica and rheumatoid arthritis. Abnormally high IL-6 levels can throw multiple physiological systems out of balance, driving the widespread systemic and joint-related damage seen in rheumatoid arthritis. Additionally, we are actively evaluating the potential of targeting the IL-33 pathway to treat COPD in former smokers, independent of their eosinophil profiles.

Key Inflammatory Pathways

Our research targets multiple biological pathways to address diverse inflammatory conditions.

IL-4 & IL-13 Pathway

Primary catalysts for type 2 inflammation. Targeted in severe asthma, atopic dermatitis, COPD, and chronic rhinosinusitis with nasal polyps.

IL-6 Pathway

Investigated for persistent inflammatory diseases including polymyalgia rheumatica and rheumatoid arthritis. High levels drive systemic inflammation.

IL-33 Pathway

Evaluated for treating COPD in former smokers, independent of eosinophil profiles. Represents a novel therapeutic approach.

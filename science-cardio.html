CARDIOVASCULAR & METABOLIC HEALTH

Driving innovative, next-generation therapies for complex cardiometabolic disorders

Conditions affecting the cardiovascular and metabolic systems rank among the most intricate and widespread chronic health challenges globally. This broad category encompasses ailments such as obesity, stroke, heart disease, fatty liver, and diabetes. Notably, cardiovascular complications remain the leading cause of mortality among non-communicable illnesses, frequently amplifying the risk for other compounding health conditions. Timely diagnosis and proactive intervention are essential for empowering patients to effectively manage their health over the long term.

Prevention Through Lifestyle

Regular physical activity and healthy lifestyle choices play a crucial role in maintaining cardiovascular and metabolic health. Our research supports comprehensive approaches that combine pharmacological interventions with lifestyle modifications.

Exercise helps improve insulin sensitivity, reduce inflammation, and strengthen the cardiovascular system. Combined with our therapeutic innovations, patients can achieve better long-term health outcomes.

Dedicated to pioneering cardiometabolic discoveries

We possess a deep-rooted legacy in cardiometabolic science, having initiated extensive clinical trials addressing severe cholesterol imbalances as early as 2009. We maintain a steadfast dedication to uncovering novel treatments for both variants of inherited hypercholesterolemia. Our diverse scientific strategies include deploying specialized antibodies engineered to regulate the proteins responsible for lipid and lipoprotein metabolism.

Our early-stage pipeline tackles cardiovascular and metabolic interventions from several innovative angles. Within the realm of obesity, we are investigating combination antibody therapies aimed at preserving lean muscle mass—a crucial factor for sustained overall health—to enhance and complement the weight reduction effects associated with GIP and GLP-1 inhibitors. Concurrently, our teams are analyzing specific genetic variations that offer natural defense mechanisms against obesity and related disorders, such as metabolic dysfunction-associated steatohepatitis (MASH).

In a distinct but highly complementary effort, we are advancing a novel therapeutic strategy for anticoagulation. The ultimate objective is to provide robust blood clot prevention while significantly minimizing the risk of adverse bleeding events.
